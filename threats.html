T H R E A T S

FACED BY TURTLES

1. Pollution

Sea turtles are sensitive to ocean pollution at all phases of development, from eggs to hatchlings to juveniles to adults. Pollutants include hazardous metals, PCBs, petroleum products, and agricultural and industrial runoff of contaminants like as fertilizers, pesticides, nutrients, and untreated garbage. Pollutants can cause immediate injury to sea turtles by direct contact, or they can accumulate in tissues over time and promote immunosuppression, ending in disease and death.
